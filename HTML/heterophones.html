LES HETEROPHONES

These are words that are spelled the same but pronounced differently, often with different meaning

Here are some of the common french heterophones:

  1. Fils - Son (pronounced /fis/) / Fils - Thread (pronounced /fil/)
  2. Est - /ɛst/: noun meaning "east." / Est - /ɛ/: verb form of "être" (is)
  3. Couvent - /kuvɑ̃/: noun meaning "convent" (a religious building) / Couvent - /kuv/: verb form of "couvrir" (they cover)
  4. But - pronounced /by/: This refers to a goal, particularly in sports / But - pronounced /byt/: This refers to purpose or aim in a more abstract sense
  5. Tous - /tus/: plural pronoun meaning "all" (pronounced when followed by a noun) / Tous - /tu/: adjective or adverb meaning "all" (pronounced when it's used alone or before a verb)
