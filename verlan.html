VERLAN

It is a form of French slang where syllables of words are reversed or altered. It is commonly used in informal settings, especially by younger people in France.

Verlan can be created by reversing the syllabes of a word.

It is important to know that some words have additional alternations, especially in more advanced verlan.

Another thing to note is that verlan is often spoken with a specific rhythm or flow, so it might sound a bit different from regular French. People tend to soften the syllables and make the new words sound more fluid.

Also, Verlan is not just for nouns; it can also be used with verbs.

A final thing to note is that Verlan is constantly evolving, so it's good to stay updated with curent trends in slang.

Here is how it works:

  1. Verlan - L'envers (meaning "the reverse")
  2. Meuf - Femme (meaning "woman")
  3. Keuf - Flic (meaning "cop")
  4. Chelou - Louche (meaning "shady")
  5. Tovoir - Voiture (meaning "car")
  6. Lieuban - Banlieue (meaning "suburb")
  7. Fric - Argent (meaning "money")
  8. Guedr - Drague (meaning "flirt")
